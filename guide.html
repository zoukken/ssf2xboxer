    • Quick overview

    Balrog (Boxer) is a top-tier character with few bad matchups. He deals high damage, has strong pokes, quick movement, powerful super, a wide throw range, and solid anti-airs. However, he can be heavily dependent on his Super, without it, he struggles against strong okizeme and zoning with projectiles.

    Pokes

    5LP
    2LP    		 - Cancellable
    - Tick throw / Frametraps
    - Punish reactively or preemptively.
    2MK - Close range
    - Meaty / Frametraps
    - Counter lows (slides, 2MK …ect)
    - Link into 2MP or Super
    2MP - Mid range
    - Cancelable
    5HP - Long range
    - Keep away move
    - Frametrap

    ⚠️ 5HP will whiff against these crouching opponents: Ryu, Blanka, Guile, Ken, Chun-Li, Cammy, and Dictator.

    Anti-air & Headbutt

    Anti-air

    - 2HP or 5MP for normals
    - Upper or Headbutt for specials

    Headbutt

    - Has invincible startup
    - Goes through projectiles
    - Sets frametraps
    - Can be safe based on range/matchup
    - LP Headbutt is the fastest but shortest
    - MP/HP Headbutt go farther but are slower

    Throw

    You should always use MP throw for its better range. In case it whiffs, standing MP comes out and works as an anti-air.

    Throw setups

    2LP,2LP > throw (or mix with a safe headbutt)
    2MP or 2MK > micro walk > throw
    On crouch: whiff Ground Upper > throw

    Throw loop mixups

    After a throw, mix by staying in front or going behind. Then, confirm your hit or do a tick throw and repeat.
    You can also fake going behind with a backstep > 2HK to bait a reversal throw.
    Mixup timing can varies by character, you should practice it for consistency.

    Throw > Safe Jump

    Hold (9) during throw (midscreen) for an auto-timed safe jump. Works on Boxer and FeiLong with jHP, and on Cammy with jLK.

    Throw > Meaty combos

    Works on Hawk, Sagat, Gief, Boxer, Honda
    - Throw > Meaty 2MK > 2HK
    - Throw > Meaty 2MK > 5HP

    Super - Crazy Buffalo

    This super has invincible startup, breaks through projectiles, and deals high damage. Be careful, though! It's unsafe from a distance, leaving a gap, and it's even more unsafe against special grabs from Gief or Honda.

  • Quicker inputs with diagonals: [1],3,1,3+P/K.
  • On block: upper (K) whiffs against crouchers
  • If safe: you can end with headbutt to keep your turn.

    • Super usages:

  • Punish unsafe moves and projectiles
  • Chip kill
  • Wakeup reversal
  • Super link (2MK) or Renda cancel with LP

    • Turn Around Punch (TAP)

    Hold then release PPP or KKK.
    TAP has 8 levels, increasing with hold time. Higher levels boost speed, range, damage, and blockstun.

  • Hold TAP before the round start for a lvl 2
  • You can use TAP before/after a special
  • Use specials by holding PPP/KKK, then release.

    • TAP lets you quickly close distance, sets up frametraps, meaties, and build meter. It's useful against zoners, rewards trades, and deals high damage.

    More infos: Fighter101 - SSF2T Balrog TAP guide - The ultimate tutorial for the Turn Around Punch

    Charge management

    Quickly chain two charge specials to improve mobility, build meter, find openings, and more.

    Headbutt > Dash

    Hold (7) to Headbutt, then go (Forward)+K/P to dash

    Dash > Headbutt

    Dash by holding (3), then go (Up)+P for headbutt.

    Upper LK > Super

    Upper LK, then quickly input 4,6+P/K.
    Diagonals (1,3+P/K) can also be used.

    Headbutt > Super

    Hold (7) to Headbutt, then quickly input 4,6+P/K.

    Practical uses
    • Dash low, then punish a jump with headbutt
    • Headbutt to avoid projectiles, then : Dash low (close the gap and find openings) OR Upper (anti-air) OR Super (punish unsafe moves)
    • After a knockdown or against a crouch-blocking opponent, whiff Ground Upper, then mix with throw, meaty, super, or headbutt.
